Tigriopus brevicornis (often called a Tigger-pod) is a coastal marine copepod.
They are a dominant member of shallow supra tidal rock pools along the North Western European coastline. 
A broad range of studies have been carried out on this species, including: its ecology, physiology, phylogeography, metapopulation genetics, development and reproductive behaviour. T. 
brevicornis has also recently been used in ecotoxicology studies and has been trialled as a live feed for larvae in several aquaculture-based studies for the past 30 years
